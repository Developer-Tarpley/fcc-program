Jupiter's Oppostion

On January 10, 2026 at 3am-MST, Jupiter will blaze in the desert sky at its brightest and closest point to Earth. Rising in the east at sunset and shining all night long, the giant planet will reveal its swirling cloud bands and four Galilean moons to anyone with binoculars or a telescope. This is the best night of the year to witness Jupiter's grandeur — cosmic show you won't want to miss.

January 10, 2026 at 3am: MST

Blue Micro-Moon

On May 31, 2026, Arizona's desert sky will host a rare Blue Micromoon. This unusual full moon is both the second of the month (a “Blue Moon”) and at its farthest point from Earth (a “Micromoon”), appearing smaller and softer than usual. Rising in the east just after sunset, it will glow all night long, offering stargazers a subtle but magical lunar show. Don't miss this chance to witness a cosmic rarity under Arizona's wide-open skies.

May 31, 2026 at 8pm-MST
